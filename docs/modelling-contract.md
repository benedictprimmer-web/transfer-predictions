## Product Scope

OWNER DECISION REQUIRED - The recommended V1 is a precision-first, abstaining, staged research prototype. It does not claim a player is genuinely undervalued from fee residual alone.

INFERENCE - The smallest defensible V1 is:

1. Timestamped player/transfer snapshot.
2. Market-consensus and negotiated-fee diagnostics for exact-date paid permanent transfers.
3. Buyer-agnostic discovery shortlist with explicit support/risk flags.
4. Buyer-specific economic value only when a named buyer, role and replacement set are supplied.
5. Conservative ranking only after uncertainty propagation.

FACT - The strict negotiated-fee candidate sample has 5,151 rows after excluding loans, frees, undisclosed fees, proxy dates, unsupported positions, missing PIT market values, suspected fees, and ages outside 18-29. Source: `reports/model-contract/component_support.csv`; command: `python3 -m validate.modelling_contract`.

## Sporting MVP Correction

FACT - The merged Sporting MVP validated a next-season minutes target. It did not validate future sporting quality, future sporting rate, total sporting contribution, transfer fee, market-consensus value, or buyer-specific economic value.

FACT - The corrected conclusion is: the tested handcrafted prior sporting-rate score did not produce stable temporal improvement over the age/role baseline for next-season minutes.

REQUIREMENT - Minutes/availability, sporting rate, total contribution, negotiated fee, market-consensus value, and buyer-specific economic value must remain separate outputs. A minutes model must not be described as a sporting-quality model, and a Transfermarkt market-consensus value must not be described as expected fee or economic value.

REQUIREMENT - Future sporting-rate targets require role-normalized metrics fitted inside each temporal training fold. If coverage is insufficient, the output is `ABSTAIN`; missing performance data must remain null.

## Feature Policy

FACT - Executable feature policy is in `reports/model-contract/feature_policy.csv`.

Allowed for negotiated-fee V1 candidate: age, supported position, origin/destination league, strictly pre-transfer market consensus, and PIT contract years only when `contract_is_pit`.

Prohibited: future performance, post-transfer market value, current-state contract snapshots, current-state wages, buyer identity in buyer-agnostic variants, encoders fit outside training folds, and final-test calibration.

## Output Semantics

FACT - Executable output policy is in `reports/model-contract/output_policy.csv`.

Fee residual must be labelled "deviation from expected negotiated fee / market-consensus pricing." It must not be labelled true player value or genuine undervaluation.

Buyer-specific surplus is unavailable without named buyer context.

Wage must be visibly flagged as observed or modelled.

## Metrics

Sporting model metrics: temporal rank correlation, minutes-weighted performance error, tier calibration, interval coverage and width, and subgroup stability. Architecture-review inclusion threshold: at least +0.03 absolute rolling rank-correlation lift or +5 percentage-point top-tier precision lift over the simpler baseline without worse interval coverage.

Fee and wage metrics: log error, absolute currency error, interval coverage, upper-tail error, era calibration, league calibration, and source-type calibration. Fee MAE alone cannot select architecture.

Recommendation metrics: top-k precision, false-positive rate, NDCG, conservative precision under lower-bound uncertainty, abstention/coverage trade-off, retrospective acquisition diagnostics, subgroup calibration. No single positive-recommendation binary label is approved yet.

## Baseline Ladder

Sporting contribution: age x role historical mean; last-season contribution with shrinkage; elastic net; CatBoost only if it materially improves temporal ranking, calibration or top-k precision.

Minutes/availability: prior-season minutes rate; age/position/injury-adjusted GLM; CatBoost challenger.

Negotiated fee: market value; age/contract/league multiplier; elastic net; CatBoost challenger.

Wage: league x club-tier x age/role median; regularized regression; CatBoost challenger.

Complexity rule: include a more complex component only if it materially improves rolling temporal ranking, top-k precision, false-positive control, subgroup calibration, interval quality, or upper-tail error without materially damaging interpretability, temporal stability, compute, reproducibility or maintainability.
