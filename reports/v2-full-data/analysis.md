# V2 Full-Data Analysis — Synthesis

Generated by `python3 -m validate.v2_full_data` on the combined
`agent/data-audit-pr` branch (post-merge, commit see
`full_data_manifest.json`). Descriptive only — no model fit, no locked-period
metric. See `docs/reconciliation.md` for the Estate-B gap this analysis
inherits (no destination-season sporting link is available in this
environment).

## 1. Coverage

FACT — `fbref_perf` covers 1,848 (season, league, role, minutes-band) cells
across seasons 2010-2026 and 5 `Comp` values. Source:
`reports/v2-full-data/coverage_cube.csv`; command:
`python3 -m validate.v2_full_data`.

FACT — of 52,951 total `fbref_perf` rows represented in those cells, 14,416
player-seasons (27%) fall in the `<450` minutes band and a further 6,699
(13%) have no parseable minutes value (`unknown` band). Source: same
artifact, `minutes_band` grouping.

INFERENCE — roughly 40% of `fbref_perf` rows are low-minutes or
minutes-unknown. Any per-90 rate computed without the shrinkage treatment
in `impact/evidence.py` would be dominated by small-sample noise for a
substantial share of the table — this is the concrete case for shrinkage
over a hard cutoff (`docs/mustermann.md` §2): a hard 450-minute cutoff
alone would discard 27%+ of rows outright rather than down-weight them.

## 2. Percentile cohort viability

FACT — 405 / 439 (92%) of (season, league, role) cells have at least 20
players and are viable for a domestic percentile at the `min_cohort=20`
threshold used in `impact/evidence.py`. 34 cells fall below that bar.
Source: `reports/v2-full-data/cohort_support.csv`.

INFERENCE — domestic percentiles are broadly viable within the 5-league
`fbref_perf` footprint, but this is a *small* footprint relative to the
134,147-row canonical transfer table (`docs/data-audit.md`) — most canonical
transfers involve at least one side outside these 5 competitions, so a
domestic percentile is unavailable for most of the full transfer estate, not
just a minority.

## 3. Metric distributions — raw vs per-90

FACT — for the five per-90-eligible count metrics tested (non-penalty xG,
xA, progressive actions, tackles, interceptions), `reports/v2-full-data/metric_distributions.csv`
reports zero-inflation and tail behaviour. All five have `n_under_450min`
in the low thousands (low-minutes rows are a large, not marginal, share of
the eligible population for every metric, consistent with §1).

INFERENCE — none of these five metrics is exempt from the shrinkage
argument in §1; the same low-minutes share affects every candidate evidence-
card dimension, not just one.

## 4. Cross-league mover support

FACT — 48 origin-destination league pairs have 5+ movers in
`transfers_canonical` (all transfer types, all seasons); those 48 pairs
cover 6,134 movers total, of which 1,146 fall in the locked period
(`season >= 2023`, correctly excluded from any fitting — see
`locked_test_audit.json`). Source: `reports/v2-full-data/mover_support.csv`.

FACT — **data-quality finding**: `transfers_canonical.from_league`/`to_league`
contains two distinct string values for the same competition —
`"La Liga"` (used 1994-2022) and `"LaLiga"` (used exclusively from
season 2023 onward, matching the competition's real-world rebrand). This
silently splits one league into two cohorts for any league-keyed grouping
(coverage cube, mover pairs, domestic percentile) that spans the rebrand
year. It also means every La Liga-linked row in the locked period
(`"LaLiga"`, 27 movers to/from Bundesliga alone) is currently invisible to
any pre-2023 "La Liga" cohort definition, and vice versa.

INFERENCE — this is a genuine defect in the canonical merge/normalization
layer (`ingest/merge.py`), not a modelling choice. It was not fixed in this
PR (out of scope of the audit-repair/V2-analysis work; fixing a canonical
warehouse column changes counts already reconciled in
`docs/reconciliation.md`). Recorded in `docs/contradiction-log.md` as a
BLOCKER-adjacent finding for whoever normalizes league names next.

## 5. Locked-period protection

FACT — `reports/v2-full-data/locked_test_audit.json`: of 134,147 canonical
transfer rows, 111,274 are development rows (`season < 2022`), 3,759 are the
deliberate `season == 2022` buffer, and 19,114 are the locked period
(`season >= 2023`), excluded by row-key hash only — no locked-row outcome
was read or computed. This audit record and the fee-prototype comparison in
`docs/v2-results.md` both route through `validate.locked_guard.dev_only()`.
